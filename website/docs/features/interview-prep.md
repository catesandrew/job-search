# Interview Prep

Walk into every interview knowing exactly what they're likely to ask — and having a polished answer ready for each one, drawn from your actual work history.

## Overview

Interview Prep is per-application. Each application gets its own prep session that considers:

- The **job description** for that specific role
- Your **linked resume** with your actual positions and bullets
- Your **experience library** with additional bullet points not on the resume

The result: questions that are relevant to *this* role at *this* company, answered with *your* real examples — not generic STAR stories.

## Generating interview prep

1. Open an application that has:
   - A job description (either manually entered or imported from URL)
   - A linked resume
2. Click the **Interview Prep** tab
3. Click **Generate Prep**
4. AI produces a ranked list of questions in ~15–30 seconds

## What gets generated

Each question card shows:

| Element | Description |
|---|---|
| **Question** | The interview question text |
| **Probability** | High / Medium / Low — how likely this question is for this role |
| **Answer** | A STAR-format answer using your actual experience |
| **Source** | "provided" badge if it came from your Known Questions list; otherwise AI-generated |

High-probability questions appear first. These are the ones the AI predicts are most likely based on the job description, company type, and role level.

## Known Questions (company-provided)

Before your interview, recruiters often send questions in advance — either explicitly ("we'll ask you these") or as a screening form. Don't leave those unanswered.

### How to add known questions

1. In the Interview Prep tab, find the **Known Questions** panel at the top
2. Type or paste a question into the input field
3. Click **Add** (or press Enter)
4. Repeat for each question you received
5. Questions auto-save as you add them

When you click **Generate Prep**, the AI:
1. **Answers your provided questions first** — using your linked resume and library
2. Then generates additional likely questions to round out your prep

Provided questions appear with a purple **"provided"** badge in the results.

### Why this matters

A company sending you questions in advance is a signal. They're telling you exactly what they care about. Answering those questions with specific, well-framed examples from your real experience is the single highest-impact interview prep you can do.

## How the AI builds your answers

The AI constructs STAR answers by:

1. Reading your linked resume — all positions, titles, bullet points
2. Reading your experience library — any additional bullets not on the resume
3. Analyzing the job description for role-specific terminology and priorities
4. Matching the question's intent to the most relevant experience
5. Framing the answer in STAR format (Situation, Task, Action, Result)

The answers are **grounded in your actual experience** — the AI cannot fabricate results it didn't see in your bullets. If a question has no good match in your history, the answer will note the gap honestly.

---

## Tips for better results

### Add your bullets to the library first
The library gives the AI more material to work with than the resume alone. Position bullets on a resume are often compressed; library bullets can be longer and more detailed.

### Be specific in your bullets
"Improved system performance" is hard to turn into a compelling STAR answer. "Reduced p99 API latency from 800ms to 120ms by replacing synchronous DB calls with Redis caching" gives the AI something to work with.

### Link a resume with a complete job description
The more specific the JD, the more targeted the questions. A vague job description produces generic questions.

### Re-generate after updating your resume
Interview prep is generated once and cached. If you update your resume or add new bullets to the library, click **Generate Prep** again to incorporate the new material.

---

## Regenerating prep

Click **Generate Prep** at any time to regenerate. The new results replace the previous set. Known Questions you've added are preserved and will be answered again in the new generation.
